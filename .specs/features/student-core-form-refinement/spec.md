# Specification

## Problem

The student core form still exposes visible legacy debt and weaker interaction trust than the rest of the refined product.

This shows up as:

- inline presentation shortcuts
- less polished action flow
- weaker continuity between steps
- a form that still feels more operational than product-grade

## Users

- Owner
- Manager
- Reception

## Requirements

### SCR-01

The main form must feel like the same product as the refined facade and financial workspace.

### SCR-02

Dominant inline debt in the target form scope must be removed.

### SCR-03

Primary and final actions must feel clear, calm, and trustworthy.

### SCR-04

The step flow must be easier to scan and understand without changing the underlying rules.

### SCR-05

Touched areas must remain lightweight and stable.

### SCR-06

Focus and keyboard behavior in the touched flow must remain understandable.

## Success

This phase is successful when:

- the target scope no longer exposes dominant inline debt
- the core form reads clearly from step to step
- action buttons and banners feel intentional instead of bolted on
- no regressions are introduced in the student registration flow
